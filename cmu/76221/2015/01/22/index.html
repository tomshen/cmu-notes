76-221 Class 4: The Odyssey

Jan 22, 2015

Zeus’s eagles (p. 98-9)

  • communication
    • with the future–the Odyssey was the first book in history
    • with the gods–confusing
      • mocking: “not all are fraught with meaning”
        • the text itself is disrespectful towards the gods
        • maybe we should pay more attention to the conviction of people
      • proven right in the end–suitors eventually all struck down
    • with the past–going to the Underworld

Why start the story with Telemachus?

  • show the home Odysseus wants to return to
  • hint at Odysseus’s character through his son
  • Telemachus too is on a journey
  • consciously done, structured

Proteus (p.135-)

  • Proteus is the Old Man of the Sea
  • known for changing form
  • “deathless gods”
  • “who rule the skies up there”–disrespectful because they could end his (Menelaus’s) suffering at any time
  • Why is Eidothea helping them against her father?
  • very immediate, human instructions for coercing Proteus
  • needed goddess’s help/advice
  • all gods have an Achilles heel
  • What does it take to pursue the truth?
    • allegory for struggling with nature for the truth
    • the struggle is worthwhile

The Underworld

  • Does asking the path lead to the truth?
  • What constitutes a person?
    • blood
  • There’s an inherent dignity in being alive (Achilles, p. 261)
